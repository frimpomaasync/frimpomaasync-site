Privacy.

What happens to your details when you fill something in here — and what happens to your customers' details if I build you a system. Written to be read, not to be survived.

frimpomaasync.com·Last updated 22 July 2026
The short version

I collect the details you type in — usually just an email, sometimes a name and phone number — and I use them to reply to you. I don't sell them, share them, or advertise to you. There are no tracking pixels and no analytics on this site. Ask me to delete you and I will, within 30 days, no questions.

This page covers frimpomaasync.com and everything on it. It's written by NaNa Frimpomaa, who is the whole company — so when it says "I", that's literal. If anything here is unclear, email hello@frimpomaasync.com and I'll answer you myself.

What I don't do.

  • No selling or sharing your details. Not to advertisers, not to data brokers, not to anyone. There is no version of my business where that happens.
  • No analytics. As of the date at the top of this page this site runs no analytics tool. I genuinely do not know who visits.
  • No advertising pixels. Nothing here is following you to another website.
  • No tracking cookies. See the next section for the small amount of storage that does happen.
  • No buying lists. If I contact you, it's because you contacted me first.

If I ever add a privacy-friendly visitor counter, I'll say so here and change the date at the top. I'm not going to add it quietly.

A one-person business has no use for your data beyond talking to you. That's not a policy position, it's just the truth of the size.

Cookies and what's stored on your device.

This site sets no tracking cookies. Two things do save small amounts of information, and both save it on your own device, not to me:

  • The chat widget. If you give it your name and contact, that sits in your own browser's local storage so the conversation makes sense. Clearing your browser data removes it.
  • Som, the free capture app. Everything you write in Som stays on your device. It's designed that way — your notes never reach me, which is also why I can't recover them for you if you clear them.

If I build you a Client Catcher.

This is the part that matters most, because then I'm handling your customers' details, not just yours. The commitments below are the same ones on the offer page, and they're written into the client agreement you sign:

  • Your customers' names, numbers and messages move between your phone, your calendar, and your follow-up system. That's the whole journey. Nothing is sold and nothing is shared.
  • Every provider that touches your customer data is named in your agreement before you sign, so you know exactly who is in the chain.
  • You own it all — the workflows, the scripts written in your voice, the documentation, and every piece of customer data in the system.
  • The system is built in your own accounts, in your name. I build and wire it for you — you never manage subscriptions, you just own them. Nothing about your business depends on me being reachable.
  • Your customer data never sits in my accounts at all. It lives only in yours. If you cancel, there is nothing of yours for me to delete — and anything I touched during the build, I no longer can, because you hold the keys.
And your obligations to your own customers

When a system texts your customers on your behalf, you are the one who owes them a privacy notice and a way to opt out. Every message sequence I build includes a working opt-out, and stopping means stopping — first request, no rescue message afterwards.

I'll walk you through what you need on your own side during the build. It isn't complicated, but it isn't optional either.

What you can ask me for.

Email hello@frimpomaasync.com and ask for any of these. There's no form, no account to log into, and no charge:

  • A copy of what I hold about you
  • A correction if something's wrong
  • Deletion — I'll do it within 30 days and confirm when it's done
  • To stop hearing from me — every email has an unsubscribe link, and replying "stop" works just as well

Depending on where you live you may have these rights by law. I'd rather just give them to you regardless of where you live, so I do.

Children.

This is a business-to-business site. It isn't aimed at children and I don't knowingly collect anything from anyone under 16. If you think I have, email me and I'll delete it.

Security, honestly.

Accounts holding client data use strong unique passwords and two-factor authentication, and access is limited to me. I'm not going to claim more than that — anyone promising a small business unbreakable security is selling something. What I can promise is that if something went wrong, I'd tell you quickly rather than quietly.

Changes to this page.

If this changes, the date at the top changes with it. If a change actually affects what happens to your details — as opposed to fixing a typo — I'll email the people on my list rather than hoping they re-read this page.
